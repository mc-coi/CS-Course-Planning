# Day 120: Assessment Review and Mid-Year Reflection

**Learning Target:** I can reflect on my learning, understand assessment feedback, and consider my progress halfway through the course.

### Overview

Today is a chance to **review your assessment results**, **understand your mistakes**, and **celebrate your progress**. By Day 120, you're halfway through the year-long course. This is a good time to step back and reflect.

### Assessment Review

Your teacher will return your graded assessments. For each problem:

1. **Look at what you got right** — What did you do well? Can you explain why?
2. **Look at what you got wrong** — What was the mistake? Do you understand the correct answer now?
3. **Identify patterns** — Did you struggle with the same type of problem multiple times?
4. **Ask for help** — If you don't understand why you lost points, ask your teacher

### Understanding Feedback

Common areas where students struggle (and what to focus on):

**Function Syntax:**
- Forgetting the colon after `def`
- Incorrect indentation
- Confusing parameters and arguments

**Return Values:**
- Printing instead of returning
- Returning but not using the return value
- Multiple returns (only first one executes)

**Scope:**
- Not realizing functions create new scope
- Forgetting that assignment creates a local variable
- Misunderstanding how global variables work

**Composition:**
- Not realizing functions can call other functions
- Difficulty tracing the order of operations
- Not capturing return values properly

**Design:**
- Writing one big function instead of breaking into pieces
- Using global variables unnecessarily
- Not using the main() pattern

**If you struggled with any of these,** review the corresponding day's notes and practice problems. Every concept can be learned with practice.

### Reflection Questions

Take time to honestly answer these:

1. **Progress:** How much have you improved since the start of the course? What's one thing you can do now that you couldn't do before?

2. **Functions:** Do you feel confident writing functions? What aspect still feels confusing?

3. **Mistakes:** What was your biggest mistake on the Unit 5 assessment? Do you understand why now?

4. **Code Quality:** How has your code improved in readability and organization over these 5 units?

5. **Problem-Solving:** Have you gotten better at breaking problems into smaller pieces?

6. **Confidence:** On a scale of 1–10, how confident do you feel about programming?

7. **Next Steps:** What do you want to improve in the second half of the year?

### Celebrating Wins

You should be proud of what you've learned. Functions are **hard**—they require you to think abstractly about code. You've mastered them (or are well on the way).

Specific wins to celebrate:
- You can write functions that other programmers can use
- You can read and understand code that's organized into functions
- You can break big problems into smaller, manageable pieces
- You can avoid common pitfalls like unnecessary global variables
- You can design clean, maintainable programs

### Common Concerns

**"I still don't really understand scope."**
- Scope is tricky. Review Days 101–102 again. The key insight: functions create new scope. Assignment creates local variables.

**"My programs feel messy."**
- That's normal! It takes practice. Look at the code from Days 113–115 (the mini-projects). Those are examples of clean programs.

**"I don't feel ready for harder programming."**
- You are. Functions are the foundation. Everything after this gets easier because you can break problems into functions.

**"I got a low grade on the assessment."**
- That's okay. Assessments are learning moments. Review the problems, understand your mistakes, and practice. Everyone improves with practice.
