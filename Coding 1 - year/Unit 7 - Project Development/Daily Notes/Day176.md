# Day 176: Project Presentations — Group A

**Learning Target:** I can present my project professionally and evaluate peer presentations with constructive feedback.

---

## Key Concepts

### Presentation Day

**For Presenters:**
- Arrive early to test your demo
- Have your slides and program ready
- Speak clearly and confidently
- Remember: audience wants you to succeed!

**For Audience:**
- Listen respectfully
- Ask thoughtful questions
- Provide constructive feedback
- Celebrate your peers' work

---

## Presentation Order & Timing

Presentations are typically 5–7 minutes each, plus 1–2 minutes for questions.

**Group A Schedule:**
- Check with your teacher for specific schedule
- Be ready when called
- Have your materials ready to go

---

## Guidelines for Presenters

**Opening:**
```
"Hi, I'm [Name]. My project is [Project Name]. It solves [Problem].
I'll show you how it works, explain an interesting part of the code,
and talk about what I learned. Let's get started."
```

**During Demo:**
- Narrate what you're doing
- Show normal usage first
- Show an edge case or interesting behavior
- Keep it to 2–3 minutes

**Closing:**
```
"That's my project. Any questions?"
```

---

## For Audience: Giving Constructive Feedback

**Good Questions:**
- "How did you handle when users enter invalid data?"
- "What was the most challenging part?"
- "Have you thought about adding [feature]?"

**Constructive Feedback:**
- "I liked how [feature] worked"
- "I was confused about [part], maybe you could explain it differently"
- "That's a clever solution for [problem]"

---

## Reflection on Peer Presentations

After presentations, you'll reflect on what you learned:

1. **What stood out to you about this project?**
2. **What coding technique did you find interesting?**
3. **How did this project compare to your own?**
4. **What would you do differently if building a similar project?**

---

## 🎯 Exit Ticket

For Presenters:
1. Did your presentation go well?
2. What questions did you get?
3. How did you feel?

For Audience:
1. What was impressive about the projects you saw?
2. What did you learn?
