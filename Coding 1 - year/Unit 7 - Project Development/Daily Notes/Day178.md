# Day 178: Course Reflection — Your Growth as a Programmer

**Learning Target:** I can evaluate my growth throughout Coding I and identify my strengths and growth areas.

---

## Key Concepts

### Reflecting on Learning

Reflection is when you look back and think about:
- What you learned
- How you've grown
- What challenged you
- What you're proud of

This reflection helps you:
- Recognize progress
- Identify patterns in how you learn
- Plan for future learning

---

## Reflection Prompts

Take time to write about each:

1. **Your Journey**
   - When did programming "click" for you?
   - What's changed about how you think since August?
   - What's one "aha moment" you remember?

2. **Your Skills**
   - What are you best at in programming?
   - What do you still find challenging?
   - How have your skills grown since Unit 1?

3. **Your Project**
   - What are you most proud of in your project?
   - What was the hardest part?
   - How did you overcome it?
   - What would you do differently?

4. **Your Growth**
   - In Unit 1, what seemed impossible that's now easy?
   - How has your confidence changed?
   - What surprised you about programming?

5. **Your Next Steps**
   - What do you want to learn next?
   - How will you continue growing?
   - What path interests you (web dev, games, AI, etc.)?

## Guided Practice

### Reflection Writing (Full Period)

**Step 1: Free Write (20 min)**
- Pick one of the reflection prompts
- Write continuously for 10 minutes (don't worry about perfect writing)
- Do the same for another prompt (10 more minutes)

**Step 2: Structured Reflection (15 min)**
- Answer each prompt thoughtfully
- Write 2–3 sentences for each
- Be honest about strengths and challenges

**Step 3: Skills Check (10 min)**
- Go through the skills list
- Check off what you're confident in
- Identify 2–3 areas for future learning

**Step 4: Create Your Portfolio (10 min)**
- Gather all your projects and code
- Document them (maybe screenshots, descriptions)
- This will be useful for college apps or job interviews!
