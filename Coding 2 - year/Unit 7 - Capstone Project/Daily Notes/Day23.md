## Audience Responsibilities

As a listener:

- **Pay attention** — Give each presenter your full focus
- **Be respectful** — No phones, side conversations, or dismissive comments
- **Ask real questions** — If you're genuinely curious, ask. Don't just ask to show off.
- **Offer compliments** — Every project is an accomplishment
- **Fill out evaluation forms** — This feedback is valuable to the presenter

---

## Evaluation Criteria Quick Reference

As you watch presentations, consider:

1. **Does it work?** — Does the app do what it claims?
2. **Is the code good?** — Organized, readable, documented?
3. **Did they test it?** — Can they explain how they tested?
4. **Did they learn?** — Can they explain the design and decisions?
5. **Is it finished?** — Could you use it as-is, or is it half-baked?

---

## Q&A Tips for Presenters

When someone asks a question:

- **Answer thoughtfully** — Don't just blurt; think first
- **Admit uncertainty** — "I'm not sure, but I think..." is okay
- **Build on feedback** — "That's a good point. I would fix that by..."
- **Short answers** — One or two sentences, then stop
- **Thank questioners** — "Great question!"

---

## Q&A Tips for Audience

When asking a question:

- **Be genuine** — Ask because you're curious, not to be difficult
- **Be specific** — "Tell me about the search feature" vs. "How does it work?"
- **Acknowledge effort** — "I liked how you... but I'm curious about..."
- **Be respectful** — Even if you found a bug: "I noticed the app crashed when... is that a known issue?"

---

## Handling Common Situations

**Presenter gets flustered or forgets:**
- It's okay. Take a breath. "Let me restart."
- Audience: stay patient and supportive.

**App crashes during demo:**
- Calmly explain: "Let me show you the code that handles this" or restart.
- Not a reflection of your grade; how you handle it matters.

**Someone asks a question you can't answer:**
- "That's a great question. I didn't implement that, but it would be a good next step."

**Awkward silence / no questions:**
- Teacher will prompt: "Does anyone have questions?" or offer feedback first.
