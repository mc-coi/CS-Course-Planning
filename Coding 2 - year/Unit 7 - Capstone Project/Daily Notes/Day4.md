# Day 4: Planning Check-In

**Session Goal:** Review your plan with teacher and peers; get feedback and approval to move into coding.

---

## Today's Focus

Before you start coding, it's time to validate your plan. You'll get feedback from your teacher and peers: Is your scope realistic? Are you missing anything? Do your classes make sense? Today is about catching planning mistakes before they become coding problems.

---

## Work Session Agenda

**Minutes 0-10:** **Teacher Mini-Lesson: Code Review as Learning**
- Why we review code and plans before implementing
- How to give constructive feedback
- What "ready to code" looks like

**Minutes 10-40:** **Individual Teacher Check-Ins (10 min each)**
- Students meet 1-on-1 with teacher
- Bring your Day 3 plan
- Teacher reviews using the **Planning Review Checklist** (below)
- Teacher gives approval to move to Sprint 1 or suggests changes
- Teacher helps unblock any questions

**Minutes 40-55:** **Peer Review (Optional)**
- Students swap plans with a classmate
- Read peer's plan and provide feedback using the **Peer Feedback Form** (below)
- Discuss: What's cool about your peer's plan? What questions do you have?

---

## Planning Review Checklist (Teacher)

Use this to evaluate whether a student is ready for Sprint 1.

- [ ] **Scope is clear and realistic** — Can reasonably be built in 20 days?
- [ ] **Core features are specific** — Not vague; clear what "done" looks like?
- [ ] **Data model is defined** — Classes/data structures make sense?
- [ ] **File structure is logical** — Easy to find and modify code?
- [ ] **Student has a plan for I/O** — How will data persist?
- [ ] **Error handling is considered** — Student aware of edge cases?
- [ ] **No major blockers** — Questions resolved or plan to research?
- [ ] **Student demonstrates understanding** — Can explain their design?

**If all checks pass:** "You're ready for Sprint 1. Start with setup on Day 5."

**If some checks fail:** Suggest specific improvements, help prioritize, consider scope reduction.

## Teacher Tips for Check-Ins

**If student scope is too big:**
- Congratulate ambition, then help prioritize
- "You have 5 features. Pick your top 3 for Sprint 1–3, and move others to 'Stretch Goals.'"

**If data model is unclear:**
- Ask them to draw a diagram or explain aloud
- Prompt: "What information does a Student need to store?"

**If student is anxious:**
- Reassure: "Planning isn't perfect, and that's okay. You'll learn as you code."
- Remind: "You have teacher support during build days. We'll debug together."

**If student is ready but over-excited:**
- Harness energy: "Great! That enthusiasm will power you through Sprint 1."
- Remind: "You start with setup tomorrow, not the fun features. Boring but important."
