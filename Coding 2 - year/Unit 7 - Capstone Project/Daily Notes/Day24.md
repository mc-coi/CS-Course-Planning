Same format and tips as Day 23:

## For Audience Today

Your role is important too:

- **Watch attentively** — Give each presenter your full focus
- **Fill out evaluation forms** — This feedback matters
- **Ask genuine questions** — If you're curious, ask
- **Offer compliments** — Every project is impressive
- **Be respectful** — Even if you see bugs or improvements

---

## Reflecting on Capstone Learning

As you listen to your peers' presentations, think:

- What did they do well that I could learn from?
- What approach did they take that was different from mine?
- What surprised me about their project?
- What would I do differently in their shoes?

---

## Common Projects You'll See

Depending on the class:

- **Data Analyzer** — Charts, statistics, data insights
- **API App** — Live data fetching, real-world integration
- **Student System** — Database-like CRUD operations
- **Algorithm Visualizer** — Sorting, performance analysis
- **Custom Projects** — Creative takes on course concepts

Each one shows different strengths and learning. Appreciate the variety.

---

## After Your Presentation (If Today)

- **Celebrate** — You did it!
- **Collect feedback** — Save the evaluation forms
- **Reflect** — What went well? What would you change?
- **Don't dwell** — If something didn't go perfectly, that's normal. You learned.

---

## Supporting Your Classmates

If a classmate's app crashed or they got stuck:

- Ask a thoughtful question that lets them show what they know
- "I liked the design of X. Can you explain how that works?"
- Help them feel successful, even if something went wrong
