# Day 9: Sprint 1 Check-In & Review

**Session Goal:** Demo your Sprint 1 work, get feedback, and identify blockers before Sprint 2.

---

## Today's Focus

It's time to step back and assess. You'll demonstrate what you've built, hear what's working well, and identify what needs to change for Sprint 2. Check-ins are a normal part of software development—even professional teams do them every few weeks.

---

## Work Session Agenda

**Minutes 0-10:** **Group Standup**
- Teacher: introduce the Sprint Review
- What is a "demonstration"? (show working features, not just code)
- Ground rules: give positive feedback, ask clarifying questions

**Minutes 10-50:** **Individual Demos (5 min per student)**
- Student runs their app and shows what works
- Narrate: "Here I'm adding a student... now I'm saving... now I'm loading..."
- Teacher & peers ask questions and take notes
- Use the **Demo Observation Form** (below)

**Minutes 50-55:** **Debrief & Planning for Sprint 2**
- Discuss: What worked? What was harder?
- Identify blockers: What got in your way?
- Sprint 2 goals: What will you build next?

## Teacher Feedback Guide

During each demo, listen for:

**Code Quality:**
- Is the app organized and easy to navigate?
- Did they handle errors (bad input, missing files)?
- Can the student explain their design?

**Features:**
- Do the claimed features actually work?
- Are there obvious bugs or crashes?
- Did they complete their Sprint 1 plan?

**Progress:**
- Is the student on track?
- Do they have momentum into Sprint 2?
- Do they understand what's next?

**Blockers:**
- What's preventing them from moving faster?
- Is it a technical skill? Unclear requirements? Scope creep?
- How can you unblock them?

---

## Sprint 1 Retrospective Questions

Answer these individually, then discuss with teacher:

1. **What went well?**
   - Which features were easy to build?
   - What part of your plan proved accurate?

2. **What was harder than expected?**
   - Which feature took longer than planned?
   - What surprised you?

3. **What would you change?**
   - If you did Sprint 1 again, what would you do differently?
   - What should you do differently in Sprint 2?

4. **What do you need help with?**
   - Technical concepts? Debugging? Planning?

---

## Transition to Sprint 2

**If your Sprint 1 is solid:**
- Celebrate! You have a strong foundation.
- Sprint 2 goal: add features and polish what you have.

**If your Sprint 1 has gaps:**
- Don't panic. This is normal.
- Prioritize: finish critical bugs before starting Sprint 2 features.
- Consider scaling down scope if needed.

**For Sprint 2:**
- Same structure: 3 days building, then review on Day 13
- Higher bar: features should integrate smoothly
- New focus: user experience and edge cases

---

## Reflection / Exit Ticket

1. What is one thing you did well in Sprint 1?
2. What is one thing you want to improve in Sprint 2?
3. On a scale of 1–10, how confident are you about finishing your project?
