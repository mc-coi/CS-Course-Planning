# Day 25: Course Wrap-Up, Reflection & Celebration

**Session Goal:** Reflect on the capstone and your growth throughout Coding 2. Celebrate your achievements.

---

## Today's Focus

You did it. You spent 25 days planning, building, testing, documenting, and presenting a capstone project. You learned real software development: planning, iteration, debugging, code review, and communication. Today we celebrate that, reflect on growth, and look forward.

---

## Work Session Agenda

**Minutes 0-10:** **Teacher Reflection & Recognition**
- Teacher: highlights from capstone projects
- Call out: interesting features, good design choices, perseverance moments
- Share: professional-world connections ("This is how real teams work")
- Thank everyone for their effort and growth

**Minutes 10-30:** **Individual Reflection Journals**

Students complete the **Capstone Reflection** (see below) independently:

- What did you build?
- What did you learn?
- What was hard? How did you overcome it?
- How did you grow as a coder?
- What are you proud of?

**Minutes 30-45:** **Small Group Share-Outs**

Break into small groups (3–4 students):

- Share one reflection with the group
- Listen to peers' reflections
- Celebrate each other

**Minutes 45-55:** **Course Reflection & Celebration**

Whole class:

- **What You Learned** (Unit-by-unit recap)
- **Growth Observations** (teacher shares what they noticed)
- **Future Steps** (what's next in CS?)
- **Celebration** (recognize effort, dedication, growth)

---

## Capstone Reflection Journal

Answer these questions thoughtfully. This is for you—be honest.

### What You Built

1. **Project Name & Overview**
   What did you build? (1–2 sentences)

2. **Core Features**
   List your 5 core features. Did you complete all of them?

3. **Biggest Feature**
   Which feature are you most proud of? Why?

### What You Learned

4. **Technical Skills**
   What technical skills did you build or improve?
   - Example: "I learned how to use APIs," "I improved my debugging skills"
   - List 3–5

5. **Software Development Process**
   What did you learn about how real software is built?
   - Example: "Testing is important," "Planning saves time"

6. **Problem-Solving**
   Describe one problem you faced and how you solved it.

### Challenges & Growth

7. **Biggest Challenge**
   What was the hardest part of the capstone?

8. **How You Overcame It**
   What did you do to get past the challenge?
   - Example: "Asked for help," "Broke it into smaller steps," "Looked at similar code"

9. **Persistence**
   Did you give up at any point? What kept you going?

### Reflection & Pride

10. **Growth as a Coder**
    How are you different as a programmer compared to Day 1 of Coding 2?
    - What can you do now that you couldn't before?
    - How is your thinking about code different?

11. **Proudest Moment**
    What moment in this capstone made you feel proud or excited?

12. **If You Did It Again**
    If you could start over, what would you do differently?

13. **Next Steps**
    What would you want to learn or build next?

## Unit-by-Unit Course Review

**Reflect on your journey:**

**Unit 1: Python Fundamentals** (Days 1–30)
- You learned variables, loops, conditionals, functions
- Why it matters: foundation for everything else

**Unit 2: Object-Oriented Programming** (Days 31–60)
- You learned classes, inheritance, methods
- Why it matters: design and organize large programs

**Unit 3: Functions & Decomposition** (Days 61–90)
- You learned how to break problems into smaller pieces
- Why it matters: makes code reusable and testable

**Unit 4: File I/O & Data** (Days 91–120)
- You learned to read/write files, parse CSV/JSON
- Why it matters: real programs work with data

**Unit 5: Lists, Dicts, Sets** (Days 121–150)
- You learned data structures and algorithms
- Why it matters: choosing the right structure makes code faster and cleaner

**Unit 6: Sorting & Searching** (Days 151–155)
- You learned algorithm complexity and optimization
- Why it matters: affects how your programs scale

**Unit 7: Capstone** (Days 156–180)
- You put it ALL together to build something real
- Why it matters: this is real software development
