# Day 23: Game Showcase — Presentations (Day 1)

**Session Goal:** Present your game to the class, play classmates' games, and give meaningful feedback.

---

## Today's Focus

Today is one of the most exciting days of the year — the game showcase! You've worked hard on this project and now you share it. Be proud of what you built. Every game, no matter the polish level, represents real learning and creativity.

---

## Work Session Agenda

**Minutes 0-5:** Set up your build or browser tab. Have your game ready to go.

**Minutes 5-50:** Half the class presents today (half presents Day 24). Each student has 4 minutes:
  - 1 minute: overview (what's the game, what inspired it?)
  - 2 minutes: live demo (play it or watch teacher/volunteer play)
  - 1 minute: reflect (what are you proud of? what would you do differently?)

**Minutes 50-55:** While others present, fill out peer evaluation forms.

---

## Presentation Guide

```
YOUR 4-MINUTE PRESENTATION OUTLINE:

Minute 1 — The Pitch (30 sec) + GDD Overview (30 sec):
"My game is called ___ and it's a ___. The player ___ to ___."
"I was inspired by ___. The core mechanic is ___."

Minute 2–3 — Live Demo:
[Play from Main Menu → Level 1 → show a win or loss moment]
TIPS:
- Practice this twice before today so you know what to show
- If it crashes — "As noted in my known bugs list..." and move on
- It's okay to narrate as you play: "Here you can see the enemy patrol..."

Minute 4 — Reflection:
"I'm most proud of ___."
"If I had more time I would ___."
"The hardest thing I built was ___."
```
